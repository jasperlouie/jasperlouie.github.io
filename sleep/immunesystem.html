Got Sleep?
Based on a study by Prather et al., called "Behaviorally Assessed Sleep and Susceptibility to the Common Cold" less sleep was correlated with an increased rate of infection when exposed to a common cold virus.
Tell me more!

By using sleep diaries, subjects tracked their sleep. They also gave themselves nasal injections of a mild form of the rhinovirus. Over a week, those who slept between 5 and 6 hours were at a greater risk than those who slept greater than 7 hours.

What about if I'm sick already?:

A study by Dickstein et al. showed that when injected with TNF-cytokine, which is a signaling protein released when one is sick, sheep tended to sleep more than they normally would. That shows that the sleepiness and fatigue associated with illness is to some degree caused by your own body, which allows us to expect that this was a result of evolution, and that sleep is important for recovery when one is already sick.
